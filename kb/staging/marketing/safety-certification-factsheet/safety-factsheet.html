2. Safety Controller SC-100 Overview

The Safety Controller SC-100 is the hardware safety backbone of every CoBot Pro system. Unlike competitors that rely on software-only safety functions or external safety PLCs, the SC-100 is a dedicated dual-channel safety processor integrated into the CoBot Pro control cabinet.

Key SC-100 Specifications

  • Architecture: Dual-channel (1oo2D), independently verified by TÜV SÜD for SIL-3 / PLe Cat 4
  • Safety response time: Less than 8 ms from hazard detection to Safe Torque Off (STO)
  • Monitored parameters: Joint position, joint velocity, joint torque, TCP position, TCP velocity, TCP force, workspace boundaries (up to 16 configurable safety zones)
  • Safety functions: Safe Torque Off (STO), Safe Stop 1 (SS1), Safe Stop 2 (SS2), Safely-Limited Speed (SLS), Safely-Limited Force (SLF), Safe Operating Stop (SOS), Safe Direction (SD)
  • Emergency stop inputs: 2× dual-channel E-Stop inputs (IEC 60947-5-5 compliant)
  • Safety I/O: 8× safe digital inputs, 4× safe digital outputs, all rated to SIL-3 / PLe Cat 4
  • Communication: Safety-over-EtherCAT (FSoE) for integration with external safety PLCs

For detailed configuration instructions, including setting up safety zones, force limits, and emergency stop circuits, see the Safety Standards Compliance guide in the Contoso Robotics Support documentation. For firmware architecture details and update procedures, refer to the Safety Controller Firmware documentation in Engineering.

3. ISO/TS 15066 Compliance Details

ISO/TS 15066 defines biomechanical force and pressure limits for human-robot contact during collaborative operation. The CoBot Pro family is tested and certified for all four collaborative operation methods defined in the standard:

3.1 Safety-Rated Monitored Stop

The SC-100 achieves Safe Operating Stop (SOS) within 8 ms, holding the arm in a zero-velocity state while maintaining motor power. Human workers can enter the collaborative workspace while the robot is in SOS mode.

3.2 Hand Guiding

The CoBot Pro supports direct hand guiding through a 6-axis force-torque sensor at the tool flange. When hand guiding mode is active, the SC-100 enforces a 250 mm/s TCP speed limit and 150 N force limit.

3.3 Speed and Separation Monitoring

When paired with external safety-rated laser scanners (connected via safe digital inputs), the SC-100 dynamically adjusts robot speed based on human proximity — full speed at distances over 1500 mm, reduced speed (250 mm/s) at 500–1500 mm, and protective stop at distances under 500 mm.

3.4 Power and Force Limiting

The CoBot Pro's default force limits comply with ISO/TS 15066 Annex A biomechanical thresholds for all 29 body regions. The SC-100 continuously monitors TCP force and joint torques, triggering Safe Stop 1 (SS1) if any threshold is exceeded. Default limits are configurable through CoBot Studio IDE within the certified range:

6. Safety Features Overview

The CoBot Pro's safety features work together as a layered defense system:

  1. Mechanical current limiting: Brushless servo motors with current limiters prevent excessive torque at the hardware level, independent of software control.
  2. Dual-channel safety monitoring (SC-100): Two independent processors cross-check all safety-critical parameters every millisecond. A disagreement between channels triggers an immediate Safe Torque Off (STO).
  3. Configurable safety zones: Up to 16 3D safety zones can be defined in CoBot Studio IDE. The SC-100 enforces speed and force limits that vary by zone, enabling different safety profiles for human-accessible and restricted areas.
  4. Collision detection: Real-time joint torque monitoring detects unexpected contacts within 5 ms. The configurable collision response can be set to stop, retract, or reduce force depending on the application.
  5. Safe skin (optional): An optional capacitive safety skin covering the arm detects human proximity within 100 mm and triggers speed reduction before contact occurs.
  6. Redundant emergency stop: Two independent E-Stop circuits with cross-monitoring ensure that a single hardware failure cannot prevent an emergency stop.
  7. Automatic safety firmware updates: The SC-100 firmware is cryptographically signed and updated through CoBot OS, ensuring safety functions always reflect the latest certified algorithms.

For detailed procedures on implementing these safety features in your specific application, refer to the Safety Standards Compliance guide in the Contoso Robotics Support documentation.
